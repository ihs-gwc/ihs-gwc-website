Guest Speaker Event

February 24, 2020

Girls Who Code at Irvington hosted an event in which speakers from various tech fields came in to talk to members of the club. The speakers were Michael K. Zhang, Harvard alumni and the co-founder/chief-instructor at AI camp, and Yatish Turakhia, a student at Stanford who worked in Microsoft, Apple, and Qualcomm. Students had the opportunity to talk to the speakers and ask questions that they had about the techfield. Zhang and Turakhia were great speakers and Girls Who Code hopes to see them again.

Field Trip to the San Jose Tech Museum of Innovation

December 14, 2019

Girls Who Code at Irvington had the opportunity to take seven members to the Tech Museum of Innovation. The students were able to get to know each other, participate in many activites, and explore the museum. Although time was limited, the members were able to walk through the whole museum and even do an activity where they grew their own bacteria. The field trip was fun and educational, allowing the students to expand their horizons.

Factory Tea Bar Fundraiser

April 18, 2019

Girls Who Code at Irvington hosted its first ever fundraiser! It was coordinated with the Computer Science Club and was hosted at Factory Tea Bar after school. The officers passed out fliers to customers allowing them to donate 20% of their purchases to Girls Who Code at Irvington. At the end of the day, the club made $80 and gained a lot of experience. This success is just the beginning and Girls Who Code at Irvington is sure to have more!
